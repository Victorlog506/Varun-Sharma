Timeline of Sir Ramanujan



  • 1887- 22 December An extraordinary Mathematician, Srinivasa Iyengar Ramanujan was born.

  • 1892- Started his schooling and he soon started excelling in his studies, especially mathematics.

  • 1897- Enrolled at Town Higher Secondary School where he discovered a book on advanced trigonometry written by S. L. Loney which he mastered by the time he was 13.

  • 1903- He got his hands on a book called ‘A Synopsis of Elementary Results in Pure and Applied Mathematics’ which is credited to have awakened the mathematical Genius in him.

  • 1904- He had independently developed the Bernoulli numbers and had calculated the Euler–Mascheroni constant up to 15 decimal places.

  • 1904- He graduated from Town Higher Secondary School and was awarded the K. Ranganatha Rao prize for mathematics.

  • 1906- He could not complete his degree from Pachaiyappa's College in Madras as he was so preoccupied in Mathmatics thus left the collage and continued to pursue independent research in mathematics.

  • 1906- After dropping out from collage, he struggled for his living and had a poor life with a poor health too.

  • 1910- He met with deputy collector V. Ramaswamy Aiyer, who had recently founded the Indian Mathematical Society. Impressed by the young man’s works and sent him to R. Ramachandra Rao, the district collector for Nellore and the secretary of the Indian Mathematical Society.

  • 1911- Rao agreed to help him get a job and also promised to financially fund his research. His first paper, a 17-page work on Bernoulli numbers, was published with the help of Ramaswamy Aiyer.

  • 1913- He was popular among the mathematical fraternity in India due to reserch papers and finally met to Godfrey H. Hardy.

  • 1914- Ramanujan travelled to England in 1914 and worked alongside Hardy who mentored and collaborated with the young Indian and also took more interest when he came to knew that without having formal training in mathematics, Ramanujan’s knowledge of mathematics was astonishing.

  • 1916- Made several advances specially in the partition of numbers. His papers were published in European journals, and he was awarded a Bachelor of Science degree.

  • 1918- Becomes Fellow of the Royal Society, Elected to Trinity College Fellowship.

  • 1919- Returns to India.

  • 1920- Dies on April 26.
